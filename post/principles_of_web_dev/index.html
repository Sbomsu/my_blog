Principles of website development

Websites have changed tremendously throughout the years. The very first websites were designed in the 1990s, it was used by scientist in research in to share recommendations between each other. Since then designers have been applying several approaches to change the way website look and feel.

Early websites were entirely text-based with limited graphic user interface and no real layout other than headings and paragraphs. The websites had only hypertext and hyperlinks as a method of navigating through text. In the present time, website can still share ideas but the web has evolved into a tool needed for the society to properly as it is ingrained into fabric of many industries. The industry has moved on in leaps and bounds since those days, bringing us table-based, Flash and then CSS designs along the way.

KEY REQUIREMENT OF ALL WEBSITES.

The following are the key requirements of all websites.

User-Friendly - It is easy to use and understand. Understanding how to produce a user-friendly website will improve the way visitors are able to interact and use your website in terms of layout,design and the content.

Consistency - To get a consistent appearance and feel, use the same layout, colour scheme, font, and images throughout the site. Ensure that logos, brand colours, and other branding components are applied consistently throughout the website. If you take a look at the London south-east college website it matches its brand colours and theme. Maintain a consistent tone and style in your writing, making sure that headings, subheaders, and body content all follow the same pattern.

Standardise the design of interactive components such as buttons, links, and forms to ensure that users know how to engage with them on different sites. Use a consistent grid system and spacing guidelines to guarantee that components are placed uniformly on the page.
Place navigation menus, footers, and other repeating components in the same place on each page.

Navigation - It is important to provide a well-organized, simple primary navigation menu that allows users to swiftly discover the website's important areas. Use navigation to display visitors' present location within the site structure and allow for easy retracing.
A search bar will help people rapidly discover certain content on the website. Ensure that all links are easily discernible and clickable, with informative anchor text indicating the destination. Consistent navigation labels throughout the site to reduce any misunderstanding.

PRINCIPLES OF WEB DESIGN

Millions of websites are built on a daily basis, so the principles of a good website design is crucial to attract visitors. This allows the website the opportunity to be successful in a market which is very competitive. It is important to note today's world even with a good principled web design you might need advertisement to attract visitors. With a bad principled website advertisement will not be effective to its potential because visitors will not enjoy a good experience as explained by in this video titled "Every Way To Get Traffic To Your Website In 2024". . A smart website efficiently includes the following components to improve user experience and engagement:

Side Layout: This means giving consideration to where elements go on a web page and how content will be spread across a website. it organises material and improves navigation, making it easier for consumers to discover what they need.

White spacing: This is the space on web page that is left untouched and white. This improves readability and concentration, resulting in a clean and professional design. Unless you utilise spacing, your web pages will look cluttered and messy,and it would be hard for visitors to interpret which words relate to which images and understand what they are looking at. Therefore, when designing a web page it is crucial to use spacing between elements so as to not overwhelm and confuse website users.

Usability: This is the ease of use of a website, which is how user-friendly it is.An important element of website usability is ensuring that websites content is flexible and works on all browsers and devices. This makes the website is simple to use, accessible, and functioning on all devices, which increases overall user happiness.

Clarity: An important element Provides information that is plain and straightforward, allowing readers to easily comprehend the site's content and purpose.

Together, these aspects create a website that is not only visually stunning, but also user-friendly and successful in meeting its objectives.
